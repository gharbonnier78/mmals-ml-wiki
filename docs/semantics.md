# Semantic contract

## Canonical biological mapping

- **Hosts** are the trees or host organisms in the biological analogy and the specialist computational modules in MMALS.
- **Fungal medium** is the mediating mycelial system and the active computational mediation layer.
- **Mycorrhiza / mycorrhizal exchange** is the mutualistic interface or relationship between the fungal medium and hosts.

These three objects must not be collapsed into one symbol, box, or definition.

## Computational mapping

| Biological term | MMALS object | What it is not |
|---|---|---|
| Host organism | Specialist encoder or functional module | The complete MMALS system |
| Fungal medium | Transform, route, allocate, remember, audit | A passive wire or softmax only |
| Mycorrhizal exchange | Governed contribution/cost relationship | A physical claim of biological equivalence |
| Carbon/resource cost | Entropy, compute, memory, latency, risk | A literal biochemical quantity |
| Growth/pruning | Adaptive connectivity or route policy | Proof of biological realism |

## Naming discipline

A biological word must always map to an explicit computational object, metric, or hypothesis. When no such object exists, use ordinary machine-learning vocabulary instead.
