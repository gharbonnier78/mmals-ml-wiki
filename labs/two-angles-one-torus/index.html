Two angles, one torus

Use the same θ and φ coordinates in a flat identification square and a projected torus.

concept: torusteaching simulation

One angle gives a circle. Two independent periodic angles give the Cartesian product S¹×S¹: a torus. The square and the donut below encode the same points.

T² = S¹ × S¹

When this matters for MMALS

A toroidal latent structure is justified only if the context or regime truly has two independent periodic coordinates. If context is categorical or unstructured, imposing toroidal geometry is decoration rather than evidence.
