Semantics

Biological mapping

A disciplined correspondence between host organisms, the fungal medium, and the mycorrhizal exchange relationship.

Canonical semanticsreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

Trees are the hosts. The mycelial network is the mediating fungal system. Mycorrhiza is the mutualistic exchange interface or relationship between them. These are three different things and must not be drawn as one object.

Engineering view

In diagrams, host modules produce local representations, the fungal medium transforms and routes signals, and mycorrhizal exchange denotes the governed interaction: contribution, cost, allocation, and reciprocal benefit. The vocabulary prevents the metaphor from obscuring architecture boundaries.

Research view

This mapping is an editorial and modeling constraint, not a biological claim that artificial modules reproduce fungal physiology. Biological terms should be translated into explicit computational objects and tested through ablations.

Formal anchor

hosts \neq fungal\ medium \neq mycorrhizal\ relationship
