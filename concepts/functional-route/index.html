Functional route

The composed route-plus-transformation computation, not merely the vector of routing coefficients.

Mechanistic supportreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

Remembering a road is not enough if the buildings and bridges along that road have changed. MMALS therefore remembers what the route computes.

Engineering view

A functional route includes route coefficients, host representations, transforms, the mediated latent mixture, and potentially downstream output behavior. Diagnostics separate route drift D_r, latent drift D_z, and output drift D_y.

Research view

The core non-implication is r_current≈r_teacher does not imply z_f,current≈z_f,teacher. This is a central causal distinction in the v0.7–v0.9 line.

Formal anchor

Current evidence

Mechanistically supported by route-function disentanglement experiments and controlled counterexamples.

Boundary

Functional preservation can still over-constrain plasticity; it must be evaluated against baselines and resource costs.
