Host specialization

Stable, causally meaningful differentiation of host roles across tasks, contexts, or functions.

Partial mechanistic supportreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

A host is specialized when it consistently does a particular useful job that other hosts do not simply replace.

Engineering view

Measure route entropy and dominance, contribution gain, ablation impact, route swaps, representation separation, route-function stability, and cross-seed reproducibility. A single heatmap is not sufficient.

Research view

Current evidence suggests a mixed ecology with hubs and partners rather than a clean one-host-per-task decomposition. Specialization is therefore an axis of analysis, not a guaranteed architectural outcome.

Current evidence

Partial support on selected tasks and host pairs; held-out functional and causal evidence has remained mixed in some Geometry-MMALS branches.

Boundary

Dominance can arise from optimization accidents, capacity imbalance, or router collapse.
