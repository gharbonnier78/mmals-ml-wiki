Geometry

Probability simplex

The constrained geometric space in which non-negative route weights sum to one.

Foundational mathematicsreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

For three hosts, every route is a point inside a triangle. Each corner means using only one host; the center means using all three equally.

Engineering view

Simplex geometry makes entropy, dominance, mixtures, barycentric coordinates, and calibrated context posteriors directly visible. Distances should be chosen carefully because Euclidean distance is not always the most meaningful probability geometry.

Research view

The simplex is a natural base space for routing and probability distributions. Information geometry, optimal transport, and energy formulations provide richer alternatives to naïve Euclidean comparisons.

Formal anchor

\Delta^{M-1}=\{r\in\mathbb R^M:r_i\ge0,\sum_i r_i=1\}

Current evidence

Mathematical foundation used by softmax routing and context posteriors.

Boundary

Representing routes on a simplex does not imply that the underlying latent manifold is simplex-like.
