Torus

The product of two independent periodic coordinates, S¹×S¹, and a candidate latent geometry only when the data truly carries two periodic degrees of freedom.

Foundational geometryreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

Two independent angles make a torus. Each angle wraps around, and together they describe every point on a donut-shaped state space.

Engineering view

The flat identification square and the 3D donut are two views of the same topology. A toroidal latent model is justified only when two independent periodic variables are present.

Research view

Toroidal structure can be probed through persistent homology, circular coordinates, reconstruction error, and stability across seeds. Imposing a torus on categorical context would be decorative rather than explanatory.

Formal anchor

T^2=S^1\times S^1
