Fungal medium

The active mediator that transforms, routes, allocates, remembers, and audits exchanges among hosts.

Implemented primitivereviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

The medium is not a cable. It decides how signals move and can change them on the way.

Engineering view

The medium contains host transforms, routing logic, cost terms, memory constraints, context handling, and observability hooks. Its outputs include mediated latent state, route vectors, contribution estimates, and drift signals.

Research view

The medium becomes scientifically meaningful only when its causal role is separated from extra capacity, generic distillation, replay, freezing, and simple mixture-of-experts effects.

Current evidence

Boundary
