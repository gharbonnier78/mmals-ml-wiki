Semantics

Mycorrhizal exchange

The governed mutualistic relationship through which hosts and the fungal medium exchange useful resources or information.

Modeling hypothesisreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

The exchange is mutualistic when using a host genuinely helps the whole system and the interaction does not simply consume resources without benefit.

Engineering view

A practical proxy removes host i from the mixture and measures the change in loss. Positive contribution gain means the host was useful for that batch or context. Costs can include entropy, latency, energy, memory, or communication.

Research view

Mutualism is not equivalent to positive average gain in one run. Strong evidence requires longitudinal benefit, resource accounting, causal interventions, and reproducibility across seeds and contexts.

Modeling hypothesis
Current evidence

Contribution-gain terms and ablations have been implemented; robust ecosystem-level mutualism remains an open research target.

Boundary

The biological word should not hide the fact that gain proxies are model-dependent estimates.
