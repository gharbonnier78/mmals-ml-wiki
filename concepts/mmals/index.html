Architecture

MMALS

A continual-learning research program in which heterogeneous hosts exchange transformed latent signals through an adaptive, auditable medium.

Implemented research programreviewed 2026-06-18stable /go/ link

In plain language

MMALS is a learning ecosystem. Several specialist hosts produce different views of an input. A mediating fungal system decides which signals to transform, combine, preserve, or weaken. The goal is not simply to imitate biology, but to test whether this structure improves continual learning, specialization, and auditability.

Engineering view

The architecture separates host encoders g_i, host-specific transformations T_i, an inferred context q(c|x), a router r_i(x,c), a mediated latent state z_f, and a readout. Its key engineering distinction from a basic mixture of experts is that the medium is treated as an active computational layer with memory, costs, contribution measurements, and observability.

Research view

The validated core should remain narrow: inferred-context routing, route-function memory, and energy/stability-aware auditing. Broader claims about biological equivalence, universal continual learning, consciousness, quantum advantage, or host ecology remain separate hypotheses. MMALS is best treated as a falsifiable architecture family plus an evidence discipline.
